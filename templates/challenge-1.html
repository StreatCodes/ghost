1. The Adaptive Firewall Crisis

The neon glow of your cyberdeck's display casts harsh shadows across your face as you crouch in the abandoned maintenance tunnel beneath Nexus Tower. Static crackling through your neural dampener, you jack into the building's network through a forgotten ethernet port—a relic from the old days that ZENITH's scanners somehow missed. Your fingers dance across the haptic interface as you probe the first layer of security, and a smile creeps across your face. This access point is running legacy protocols from before Nexus fully integrated their systems with ZENITH. The authentication gateway displays crude ASCII art and text-based prompts—ancient by current standards, but also refreshingly straightforward.

"Ghost, what's your status?" crackles through your encrypted comm from Cipher, the resistance's mission coordinator. "I'm in luck," you whisper back, studying the vintage security system. "This old maintenance access still uses pre-AI authentication. It's basic cryptographic challenges and logic puzzles—the kind of thing they used to test junior programmers with." You recognize some of the patterns from your early days at Nexus, back when human ingenuity still mattered more than algorithmic brute force.

The system presents its challenge: a series of encoded messages and logical sequences that need to be decoded to gain access to the building's internal network. It's almost nostalgic, like solving puzzles from the old days when hacking was about wit and creativity rather than AI-assisted warfare. But there's no time for nostalgia—once you're past this gateway, you'll have access to the lower-security internal systems that could provide a pathway to Sector 7. The resistance is counting on you, and this vintage puzzle is your first step toward uncovering Nexus's dark experiments.

As you interface with the ancient terminal, lines of seemingly random ASCII characters scroll across the screen. Your old Nexus training kicks in—you recognize this as a legacy authentication cipher that the early programmers used to secure maintenance access points. The system waits patiently for you to decode the character sequences and provide the correct numerical key.

You recall the rules of the cipher system as followed:

  • Letters (A-Z, a-z) = their alphabet position (1-26), case insensitive
  • Digits (0-9) = their numeric value
  • Any other characters = 0

    • Here are some examples:

    123 a@c6d m9(02)gc2
  • The first line would equal 6 - 1 + 2 + 3 = 6
  • The second line 14 - 1 + 0 + 3 + 6 + 4 = 14
  • And the third 36 - 13 + 9 + 0 + 0 + 2 + 0 + 7 + 3 + 2 = 36

    • Task:

    Calculate each line's total and add all the totals together. In the example above the final total would be 56
